Swedish Government

Sweden's government is defined as a parliamentary representative democratic constitutional monarchy. Which basically means they are a democracy that votes for a monarch.

Types of Land Organization

Nation-State: A place that the ethnic and state boundaries coincide.

Multi-National State: A Stat that is home to more thatn one autonomous nation.

Stateless Nation: A Nation spread throughout different states while being a minority in them all.

Multi-State Nation: A nation that spreads accross multiple different states and has control over at least one.

Sweden is a Nation-State

Boundaries

Subsequent Boundaries: A boundary established after settlement of the area. An example of this would be the Finland-Sweden Border, which was established in early settlement but has had changes since.

Consequent Boundaries: A type of subsequent boundary that is created after settlement to accomodate cultures and ethnicites. An example of this would be the small changes made the the Finland-Sweden border to help accomodate people with national ties to one country but living in another.

Government

Federal Government: A government that divides power between a central government and local government units.

Unitary Government: A government with power centralized at the central state government.

Sweden is a federal government leaning towards unitary state.
